# Client Advisory Board Report — May 2026

Six clients, 90 minutes, recorded with permission. The brief: walk the
portal as you would on a real Tuesday morning. No facilitator prompts
beyond "what are you trying to do right now?" We leaned deliberately
toward voices who are *not* power users — the third user.

## Findings, by persona

### Marie — got stuck on the front door

- Liked the eyebrow labels ("ENGAGEMENT" / "DECISION") — told her what
  she was looking at.
- Got lost finding the home page twice. Workspace switcher was hidden
  among the profile chrome.
- Wrote down "what is canonical?" in her notebook. Tried to translate
  the page in French.
- Found the **Signed export** button on `/portal/governance` and was
  visibly relieved — but didn't click for fear of breaking something.
- **Wanted:** plain-language tooltips, bilingual mode, "show me what
  this button does without doing it" affordance.

### Sanjay — wanted the punchline

- Skimmed `/portal` in 12 seconds. Clicked the first number tile.
- Read the first sentence of the Executive Summary and stopped.
- "What does 30 events tell me? Are 30 a lot?"
- Never opened the simulator. Never clicked into a single decision.
- **Wanted:** email digest, "your three decisions this week" landing
  card, trend deltas next to every number.

### Lena — pattern-matched to past trauma

- "This looks like Workday." Not a compliment.
- Quietly anxious walking through `/portal/agents`. "Are these things
  doing my job?"
- Loved `/portal/knowledge` — "Oh, this is just a library."
- Alarmed by `/portal/actions`. Clicked dry-run, was surprised when
  nothing happened.
- **Wanted:** photo/initial next to every agent so it's clearly a tool;
  plain-language dry-run explainer; "send a comment to a human" with
  parity weight.

### Marcus — the only one who clicked the small print

- Direct to `/portal/governance` + `/portal/compliance`.
- Generated a Signed export. Opened it. "OK, that's a real signature."
  Wanted to know where the key lives and how to rotate.
- Wanted posture visible on the governance page itself, not buried in
  audit findings.
- Rate limiter was invisible to him.
- **Wanted:** "Controls in force" panel. Per-connector workspace
  approval before any connector goes live.

### Priya — the daily user

- Liked the **AUTONOMOUS ENGINE** card immediately. Read three
  proposals aloud.
- Wanted action buttons inside each proposal — not just descriptive
  text.
- Tried to filter the Pattern Library: free-text returned fewer results
  for longer queries. Confusing.
- Tripped over Markdown vs. Signed bundle labels. Picked the signed
  one because it had more letters.
- **Wanted:** click-to-act buttons in proposals; click-to-filter chips;
  differentiate the two export buttons; web preview for signed bundles.

### Jim — closed the tab at minute three

- Opened Chrome at 150% zoom. Three tiles overflowed.
- Clicked once, didn't understand, closed the tab. Asked: "Can I get
  this as a PDF every Monday?"
- "Agent" and "human" as actor kinds confused him until Priya
  explained.
- Logged in as "executive" with no idea what that meant.
- **Wanted:** printable weekly digest; larger default type; plain-
  language role labels; one-page glossary linked from every page footer.

## Cross-cutting themes

1. **Vocabulary alienates.** Canonical, archetype, bookshelf,
   handoff, NBCA — none of these clients knew what they meant.
2. **No "what should I do today?" surface.** Sanjay wanted three
   items. Marie wanted "what's blocking my brief." Priya wanted the
   engine output to be actionable.
3. **Trust gaps around AI actions.** Lena worried about agents
   acting *without* her. Marcus worried about agents acting *without
   authorization*. Both wanted a "controls in force" panel.
4. **Signed export underexplained.** Real differentiator, currently
   described like a hash function.
5. **Actions are too far from insights.** Proposals describe what to
   do but don't *let* you do it.
6. **The Outbound Actions sandbox needs a translator.** Two clients
   didn't know what they were looking at. One wanted per-connector
   approval before anything ships.

## Strategic blind spots

1. **The portal optimizes for the first user.** Priya thrives;
   Marie, Lena, Jim drift. The high-performer-first design loses the
   daily users who decide whether the tool gets used at all.
2. **No story for "something went wrong."** Lena: "If a bot books a
   meeting on my calendar that shouldn't be there, what do I do?"
   No surface answers this question in under three clicks.
